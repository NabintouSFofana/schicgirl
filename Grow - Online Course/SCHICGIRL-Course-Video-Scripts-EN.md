## 🎬 l1.mp4 — How Type 4 Hair Actually Grows
```
Let's start with the truth nobody told you clearly: your hair grows. It always
has. Every follicle cycles through growth, a short rest, and shedding — and on
average hair grows about half an inch a month, no matter your texture. So why does
Type 4 hair seem stuck? Because our tight coils make the ends drier and more
fragile, so they break as fast as they grow. That's not a growth problem — it's a
retention problem. And retention is something you can control.
```

## 🎬 l2.mp4 — Why Your Hair Seems Stuck
```
If your hair's been the same length for months, that's not a curse — it's a
pattern. Usually it's a handful of quiet habits stealing your length: rough
detangling, too much heat, skipping moisture, neglecting your ends, over-styling.
None of them feel dramatic in the moment, but together they erase your progress.
In this lesson we'll name each one — because once you can see what's breaking your
hair, you can stop it.
```

## 🎬 l3.mp4 — The Scalp Is Everything
```
Healthy hair starts where you can't even see it — your scalp. Think of it like soil:
nothing grows well in neglected ground. Your scalp has its own microbiome, and when
we over-wash, pile on harsh products, or never massage it, we throw it off balance
and slow growth down. In this lesson I'll show you how to clean it gently, stimulate
circulation, and treat your scalp like the foundation it is.
```

## 🎬 l4.mp4 — Moisture, Protein & Balance
```
Most "my hair won't grow" problems are really a balance problem. Moisture keeps
your hair soft and flexible; protein keeps it strong. Too much moisture and your
hair feels mushy and stretchy. Too much protein and it feels hard and snaps. The
magic is in the balance between the two. In this lesson I'll teach you to read what
your hair is telling you, so you give it what it actually needs.
```

## 🎬 l5.mp4 — Diet, Sleep & Stress
```
Your hair grows out of your body, so what happens inside matters. Protein, water,
iron and healthy fats feed your follicles. Deep sleep is when your body repairs.
And chronic stress? It can literally push hair into shedding early. You don't need
to be perfect — but in this lesson we'll cover the simple inside-out habits that
support every other thing you do for your hair.
```

## 🎬 l6.mp4 — Patience: The Hardest Skill
```
I'll be honest with you — patience is the hardest part of this whole journey. Hair
grows about half an inch a month, which means real, visible change takes time. The
women with the longest, healthiest hair aren't doing something magic; they're just
consistent over months and years. In this lesson we'll reframe patience, not as
waiting, but as trust in a process that's already working.
```

## 🎬 l7.mp4 — Consistency Over Perfection
```
You do not need the perfect routine. You need a simple one you'll actually repeat.
I'd rather you do three good things every week for a year than ten perfect things
for two weeks and then quit. Consistency is what compounds. In this lesson we'll
build habits small enough that you can't fail — because a routine only works if
you keep showing up for it.
```

## 🎬 l8.mp4 — Your Chapter 1 Is Not Her Chapter 10
```
Comparison will steal your joy faster than anything. That woman online with waist-
length coils? You're seeing her chapter ten. You're at chapter one — and that's
exactly where she started. Your hair, your texture, your timeline are your own. In
this lesson we'll let go of the comparison trap and focus on your progress, against
your own past, not anyone else's highlight reel.
```

## 🎬 l9.mp4 — Your Emotional Relationship With Your Hair
```
For so many of us, hair carries history — childhood comments, harsh salon chairs,
years of being told our texture was "too much." That story shapes how you treat
your hair today. In this lesson we'll gently unpack that relationship, because when
you stop fighting your hair and start caring for it with love, everything — your
routine and your results — changes.
```

## 🎬 l10.mp4 — Confidence & Realistic Expectations
```
Confidence and realistic expectations go hand in hand. When you understand what's
actually possible for your hair — and let go of the fantasy timelines — you stop
feeling like you're failing and start enjoying the journey. In this lesson we'll
set goals that are ambitious and kind, so you can feel proud of your crown at every
single length, not just the finish line.
```

## 🎬 l11.mp4 — Building Your Growth Routine
```
Now we get practical. A growth routine has just a few pillars: cleanse your scalp,
deep condition for moisture, seal it in, protect your ends, and stimulate the scalp.
That's it. In this lesson I'll help you build a weekly routine around your real life
— your schedule, your hair, your goals — so it's something you'll genuinely keep.
```

## 🎬 l12.mp4 — Protective Styling for Retention
```
Protective styles are one of your best tools for keeping length — but only when
they're done right. Too tight, left in too long, or neglected underneath, and they
do more harm than good. In this lesson I'll show you how to protect your ends, keep
your scalp cared for, and use styles to retain length instead of losing it.
```

## 🎬 l13.mp4 — Ingredients That Actually Work
```
The product aisle is overwhelming, and most of it is marketing. So let's cut
through it. A few ingredients genuinely support Type 4 growth and retention —
humectants to draw in moisture, rich butters and oils to seal, and scalp
stimulants like rosemary. In this lesson I'll teach you to read a label and know
what's worth your money and what's just a pretty bottle.
```

## 🎬 l14.mp4 — What to Stop Immediately
```
Sometimes growth is less about what you add and more about what you stop. Daily
heat, tight styles on your edges, rough detangling on dry hair, cotton pillowcases,
skipping wash day for weeks. In this lesson we'll go through the habits quietly
sabotaging your length — and the gentle swaps that protect it starting today.
```

## 🎬 l15.mp4 — Tracking Your Progress
```
You can't improve what you don't measure. Styled hair hides your real length, so
it's easy to feel like nothing's happening when it actually is. In this lesson I'll
show you simple ways to track — stretched length photos, measurements, and notes on
how your hair feels — so you can see your progress clearly and stay motivated.
```
