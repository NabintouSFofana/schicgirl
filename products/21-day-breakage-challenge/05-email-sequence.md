## EMAIL 2 — Day 1 (Baseline Photos)

**Subject:** Today's the only "before" you'll ever take — let's get it right

Today is your baseline. It takes 10 minutes, and it's the most important day of the whole
challenge — because you cannot prove a change you never measured.

**Do these three things today:**

**1. The comb test.** Comb through a dry section the way you normally would. Look at what comes
out. Snap a photo of it. This is your Day 1 comb test — you'll repeat it on Day 21 and the
difference will tell the story.

**2. Spot the difference.** A **broken** hair is a short little fragment with *no* white bulb at
the end. A **shed** hair is full-length with a tiny white bulb at the root — and shedding 50 to
100 a day is completely normal and healthy. We're only here to fix the broken pieces.

**3. Log it.** Open your tracker and write down what you see. Honest numbers, even if they sting.
Especially if they sting.

That's it for today. No new routine yet — just an honest look at where you're starting.

> **[ Log your baseline in the tracker → ]**

You're doing the brave part first,
Schicgirl

## EMAIL 3 — Day 7 (First Wash-Day Breakage Check)

**Subject:** Your first wash-day check — what's in the pile?

You've made it a full week. By now the gentle-handling moves should be starting to feel less
awkward and more like *yours*.

This email is timed to your **first wash day** of the challenge — whether you wash weekly or every
10 to 14 days, do this check on whatever day your wash actually lands. There's no "right"
schedule; there's only *your* schedule.

**On your next wash day:**

**1. Detangle the right way.** On **damp**, conditioner-saturated hair — not soaking wet.
Soaking-wet hair is at its weakest and snaps easily; damp hair with conditioner has the slip that
lets the comb glide instead of tear. Fingers first. Then your wide-tooth comb. Four sections.
Always ends to roots — never roots to ends.

**2. Save the pile.** Collect what comes out and photograph it. This is your Day 7 wash-day photo.
Into the tracker it goes.

Don't judge the size of it yet — Day 7 is still early, and this is your first real comparison
point, not your finish line. We're building the data.

> **[ Add your Day 7 photo to the tracker → ]**

Proud of you for showing up,
Schicgirl

## EMAIL 4 — Day 14 (Compare D7 vs D14)

**Subject:** Put the two piles side by side. Look closely.

Two weeks in. This is where it starts to get good — because today you have something to *compare against.*

**On this week's wash day, run the same check:** detangle on damp, conditioner-saturated hair.
Fingers, then wide-tooth comb, four sections, ends to roots. Collect the pile. Photograph it.
That's your Day 14 wash-day photo.

**Now the part that matters:** open your tracker and put your **Day 7 photo next to your Day 14 photo.**

Look at them side by side. For a lot of women, this is the moment it clicks — the pile is smaller.
Fewer short broken fragments. Less hair lost to the comb. That shrinking pile *is* the win. That's
length you're now keeping instead of leaving in the sink.

If the piles look about the same, don't lose heart — check your technique against the workbook
(damp, not soaked; ends to roots; gentle hands) and keep going. The biggest shift often shows up
by Day 21.

## EMAIL 6 — RECOVERY (triggered after a missed day)

**Subject:** You didn't fall behind. Pick up right here.

I noticed it's been a couple of days. First thing: **nothing is ruined, and you owe no one an apology.**

Life happened. It happens to everyone who's ever done one of these — including me. The women who
keep their length aren't the ones who never miss a day. They're the ones who come back.

So here's the rule, and it's the kindest one in the whole challenge:

**You do not start over from zero.** You pick up on the day you left off. If you did Day 4 and life
swallowed Days 5 and 6, you start again at Day 5. Your baseline photos from Day 1 still count. Your
progress still counts. *You* still count.

Type 4 hair doesn't need perfection. It needs consistency over time — and consistency includes the
coming-back.

Open your tracker. Find your last completed day. Do the next one today. Five minutes. That's all I'm asking.
