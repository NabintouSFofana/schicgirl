### POST 2 — Broken vs Shed Myth-Buster · *Goal: Engagement*
**Hook:** Pick up one of those hairs off your shoulder. I'll tell you if it's a problem.

Not all hair on your shirt means the same thing. Do this little test:

🔍 **Look at the end of the strand.**

➡️ Tiny white/clear bulb at the root end + full length = a SHED hair. Completely normal. You shed
50 to 100 a day. Nothing to fix.

➡️ Short little fragment with NO white bulb on either end = a BROKEN hair. A piece that snapped off
mid-strand. THAT is the one stealing your length.

So before you panic at hair in the sink, check the strand. Sheds are normal. Fragments are the
warning sign. Once you see fewer fragments, your ends are getting stronger — and your length is
staying on your head.

The 21-Day Challenge teaches you exactly how to read this and reduce those fragments. Want in? Drop a "🔍".

### POST 3 — Stop Soaking Your Hair · *Goal: Engagement*
**Hook:** If you detangle in the shower under running water, this is why your ends keep snapping.

Most of us were taught to detangle when our hair is soaking wet, raking a comb top to bottom. It
feels easier because the water makes everything slip.

But soaking-wet Type 4 hair is at its most fragile and most swollen. Comb through it hard in that
state and you're not removing tangles — you're snapping strands.

Try this instead:
💧 Detangle on **damp** hair, not soaking. Damp, not dripping.
🧴 Saturate with conditioner first so strands have real slip.
🖐️ **Fingers first** — separate big tangles by hand before any tool.
⬆️ Work from the **ends up** to the roots — never roots to ends.

Slower hands at the ends = fewer fragments in the sink. This one change protects more length than
any product on the shelf. It's one of the 5-minute habits inside our 21-Day Challenge. Comment "DETANGLE".
