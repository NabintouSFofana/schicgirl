# Strategy & Concept

## Brand CEO verdict: APPROVED (revised)

**Positioning insight:** "It's not your growth, it's your retention." Perceived "no growth" in
Type 4 hair is almost always a retention failure, not a growth failure.

**The boundary that protects the prime directive (one product = one transformation):**
- **Grow (core ebook)** owns the *knowledge* — the growth cycle, why Type 4 breaks, the complete
  method and techniques. The reference library.
- **The 21-Day Challenge** owns *implementation and habit formation* — daily actions, a tracker,
  accountability. It teaches almost no new theory. If a concept needs explaining, it points to Grow.

**Ladder:** activation layer beside Grow. Entry → Grow → Type 4 Hair Routine System (flagship).

**Two upsell paths:**
1. Entry buyer → Grow ebook (the full why + system).
2. Grow buyer → Challenge as activation (knowledge they own but haven't acted on).
3. Both → Type 4 Hair Routine System at Day-21 graduation.

---

## Concept brief (Product Creator)

**Problem:** Type 4 women are losing the length they already grow. Hair grows at the root but
snaps at the ends before it shows as length. They don't need more information — they need to
*do the routine consistently* long enough to see less hair in the comb.

**Transformation:** From hair in the comb, snapped ends, and stalled length → to visibly less
breakage and a retention routine that runs on autopilot. The honest win is **reduced breakage +
a routine that sticks**, never length gain.

**Format:** Digital 21-day action workbook (PDF) + printable tracker + 6-email cadence.
Bilingual FR/EN to match the brand pipeline.

## Content-ownership map (proves non-overlap with Grow)

| Topic | CHALLENGE owns (what to DO) | GROW owns (the why) — referenced only |
|---|---|---|
| Why hair breaks vs sheds | One-line spotting cue on the tracker | Full science of breakage vs shedding |
| Daily anti-breakage actions | The full 21-day protocol | — |
| Detangling | The method (damp, slip, fingers→comb, 4 sections, ends→roots) | Why friction/dry manipulation snaps strands |
| Moisture & sealing | The act: mist + seal the ends | LOC/LCO theory, porosity, product selection |
| Protective styling | Do one low-tension style, tuck ends | Tension theory, style catalog |
| Ends / split ends | Search-and-destroy action, book a trim | Why splits travel up the shaft |
| Wash-day method | The action sequence + breakage check | Full regimen design, frequency logic |
| Measuring progress | Comb test + breakage photos | Growth-rate science, retention math |
| Habit formation | The chain, milestones, non-negotiables, cadence | — (net-new behavioral IP) |

**Rule enforced everywhere:** every Challenge cell is a verb; every Grow cell is an explanation.
Any "but why?" is answered with "→ Grow p.X," never a paragraph.

## Accountability mechanism
1. **The unbroken chain** — 21 checkboxes; the visible gap of a missed box is the pressure.
2. **3 win milestones** — wash-day breakage checks at Day 7 / 14 / 21 (dated photo proof).
3. **The restart clause** — miss a day, resume where you left off; never start over (kills the
   #1 challenge-killer: shame after a missed day).
4. **Email cadence** — Day 0/1/7/14/21 + a triggered recovery email.
5. **Graduation hook** — Day-21 completion → flagship Routine System.
