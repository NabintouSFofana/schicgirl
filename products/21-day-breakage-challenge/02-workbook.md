## FRONT MATTER

### Welcome — The Honest Promise
In 21 days you will **reduce your breakage** — less hair in the comb, fewer snapped ends, a
smaller wash-day pile — and build a 5-minute routine that **sticks**.

What will **not** happen in 21 days: your hair won't get dramatically longer. Nothing makes hair
grow faster — it grows about 1–1.5 cm a month from the scalp, for everyone. What you *can* change
is how much of that growth you **keep**. That's the whole game, and that's what we're here to win.
*(What retention actually is → Grow p.X)*

### The One Rule
This book tells you what to **DO**. Every time you wonder *why*, look for **→ Grow p.X**. You'll
never be left under-informed — the deep answers all live in one place.

### How the 21 Days Work
- **Week 1 — Stop the damage at the source.** You'll stop *causing* breakage.
- **Week 2 — Protect & seal the ends.** You'll defend the oldest, most fragile hair.
- **Week 3 — Lock in the habit.** You'll make it automatic so the result outlasts the challenge.

### How to Use the Tracker + The Unbroken Chain
Log five quick things each day (see [03-tracker.md](03-tracker.md)). Fill one box of the 21-box
chain per day. Hit three milestones (Day 7, 14, 21).
**The restart clause:** miss a day? You do **not** restart the 21. Mark it, re-do yesterday's
action, and keep the chain. Consistency includes the coming-back.

### Your Baseline Kit Check (get these before Day 1)
- A wide-tooth comb
- A regular conditioner (any you own)
- A sealing oil or butter
- A spray/mist bottle with water
- Satin bonnet, scarf, or pillowcase
- Sharp hair shears
- Your phone (for photos)
- 5 minutes a day

### Photo Standardization (this makes your proof real)
Every progress photo: **same spot, same light, same white surface, same distance.** Without this,
your Day 7 / 14 / 21 comparisons are just noise.

## WEEK 1 — STOP THE DAMAGE AT THE SOURCE
*This week's win: you'll stop causing breakage.*
*Minimum if today is hard: bonnet on tonight beats nothing.*

### Day 1 — Baseline
- **Action:** Take 2 photos (full length + ends close-up) and do the comb test: comb one slow
  pass over a white surface, count/photograph what falls. Log it.
- **Time:** 15 min
- **Do it like this:** Comb one full pass, photograph the pile against white. This is the "before"
  that makes your "after" mean something.
- **Spot it:** A **broken** hair is a short fragment with **no** white bulb. A **shed** hair is
  full length with a tiny **white bulb** at the root — normal (50–100/day). We only fix the broken ones.
- *→ Grow p.X (breakage vs shedding)*

### Day 2 — Retire the breakage tools
- **Action:** Remove every breakage tool from rotation — fine-tooth/rat-tail combs, brushes used
  on dry hair, rough towels, snagging elastics. Bag them for 21 days.
- **Time:** 10 min
- **Do it like this:** Physically set them aside so you can't reach for them on autopilot.
- *→ Grow p.X (tools & mechanical damage)*

### Day 3 — Hands off dry hair
- **Action:** Go the whole day without picking, pulling, or restyling dry hair. Touch only to moisturize.
- **Time:** passive (still counts — log it)
- **Do it like this:** If you must touch it, mist first. Dry Type 4 hair snaps under tension.
- *→ Grow p.X (dry-hair fragility)*

### Day 4 — Detangle the right way
- **Action:** Detangle **only** on **damp**, conditioner-saturated hair — damp enough to have
  slip, **not** dripping wet. Fingers first, then a wide-tooth comb, in 4 sections, ends-to-roots.
- **Time:** 20–30 min
- **Do it like this:** Soaking-wet hair is at its *weakest* — the safety comes from the
  conditioner *slip*, not from water volume. Ease knots with fingers first, comb second, always
  ends → roots, never roots → ends.
- *→ Grow p.X (why slip prevents snapping)*

### Day 5 — Switch to satin at night
- **Action:** Sleep on satin tonight — bonnet, scarf, or pillowcase. Non-negotiable from here on.
- **Time:** 5 min
- **Do it like this:** All hair tucked, edges covered. Satin reduces the friction that snaps ends overnight.
- *→ Grow p.X (friction at night)*

### Day 6 — Restyle once, slowly
- **Action:** Restyle once today using only fingers or a wide-tooth comb, on damp (not dry) hair.
- **Time:** 20 min
- **Do it like this:** Wet first, never force a tangle, no rushing. Slow hands keep length.

### Day 7 — WASH DAY + Breakage Check ⭐ (Milestone 1)
- **Action:** Gentle, sectioned wash (conditioner detangle, no fine combs). Collect and photograph
  the day's breakage pile against white — this is your **Week-1 benchmark**.
- **Time:** your wash routine
- **Do it like this:** Don't judge the size yet — it's your first data point, not the finish line.
- **Flex note:** If you wash every 10–14 days, that's perfectly healthy — do this check on *your*
  wash day nearest Day 7. Don't add a wash to hit a date; the goal is less manipulation, not more.
- *→ Grow p.X (wash-day handling)*

## WEEK 2 — PROTECT & SEAL THE ENDS
*This week's win: you'll protect the oldest, most fragile hair — your ends.*
*Minimum if today is hard: mist + seal your ends before bed.*

### Day 8 — Find your driest section
- **Action:** Locate the section that feels roughest/crispiest (usually the back/nape). This is
  your priority zone this week.
- **Time:** 5 min
- **Do it like this:** Run fingers down each section; mark the worst.
- *→ Grow p.X (porosity & dryness)*

### Day 9 — Mist + seal the ends
- **Action:** Mist hair with water or a water-based leave-in until lightly damp, then seal —
  focusing on the **ends** (the oldest, most fragile part). Smooth a small amount of oil/butter
  down the length, concentrating on the last few inches.
- **Time:** 10 min
- **Do it like this:** Water (or leave-in) first, oil/butter on top to lock it in — oil over plain
  water beads off. Ends are the priority, but on very dry/4C hair the mid-shaft is thirsty too.
- *→ Grow p.X (your exact oil/butter depends on porosity — full breakdown there)*

### Day 10 — Low-tension protective style, ends tucked
- **Action:** Wear one low-tension style that tucks the ends away (loose twists, bun, mini-twists
  — whatever you can do).
- **Time:** 20–40 min
- **Do it like this:** Low-tension = **zero pulling on your edges or roots.** If it stings, feels
  tight, or raises little bumps along your part, it's too tight — loosen it. Ends should not be
  exposed to friction.
- *→ Grow p.X (protective styling & tension)*

### Day 11 — Search & destroy split ends
- **Action:** Find frayed/split strands and snip just above the split with **sharp** hair shears.
  Aim for **up to ~10** — a cap, not a quota.
- **Time:** 15 min
- **Do it like this:** Sharp shears only (dull/kitchen scissors crush the cuticle and create new
  splits). If you can't find 10, that's a good sign. This is search-and-destroy, not a trim.
- *→ Grow p.X (how splits travel up the shaft)*

### Day 12 — Re-seal check + log moisture
- **Action:** Touch your ends. Dry? Mist + seal again. Log moisture level (Dry / OK / Soft).
- **Time:** 10 min
- **Do it like this:** Ends should feel soft, not crispy. Hydrated ends don't snap.

### Day 13 — Low-tension 24 hours
- **Action:** Keep hair in a low-tension, protected state for 24 hours — no tight ponytails, no
  edge-pulling styles, no bands cutting in.
- **Time:** passive (still counts — log it)
- **Do it like this:** Hands off; ends stay tucked. One day of protective wear is low-risk; just
  keep it loose.

### Day 14 — WASH DAY + Breakage Check ⭐ (Milestone 2)
- **Action:** Wash, deep-condition focusing on ends, protective-style while damp. Photograph the
  pile and put it **side by side with your Day 7 photo.**
- **Time:** your wash routine
- **Do it like this:** Same white surface, same lighting. A smaller pile = it's working. If the
  piles look the same, check your technique (damp not soaked; ends to roots; gentle hands) and keep going.
- *→ Grow p.X*

## WEEK 3 — LOCK IN THE HABIT
*This week's win: you'll make it automatic — so the result outlasts the challenge.*
*Minimum if today is hard: run your night routine; that's the keystone.*

### Day 15 — Name your 3 non-negotiables
- **Action:** Write the 3 actions from Weeks 1–2 that helped most. These become your forever routine.
- **Time:** 10 min
- **Do it like this:** Pick the 3 you'll never skip. Write them on the recap card (back matter).

### Day 16 — Night routine on autopilot (<5 min)
- **Action:** Run your night routine in under 5 minutes — satin + pineapple/loose protected style
  + sealed ends. Repeat nightly to Day 21.
- **Time:** <5 min
- **Do it like this:** Same order every night, no decisions.

### Day 17 — Stock your kit
- **Action:** Replace/restock anything running low — satin, sealing oil/butter, shears, mist bottle.
- **Time:** 15 min
- **Do it like this:** A routine you can't supply is a routine you'll skip.

### Day 18 — Schedule your next 2 wash days
- **Action:** Put your next 2 wash days on a calendar with reminders.
- **Time:** 5 min
- **Do it like this:** Real dates. The routine has to outlive the challenge.

### Day 19 — Book a trim / dusting
- **Action:** Book a trim, or plan an at-home dusting (just the very tips, ~3 mm).
- **Time:** 10 min
- **Do it like this:** A dusting is **not** losing length — it's protecting it. A split end keeps
  splitting upward until the strand snaps higher up; dusting the frayed tip now stops a bigger,
  unplanned break later. If your ends look healthy and you did Day 11, you can skip this.
- *→ Grow p.X (trimming for retention)*

### Day 20 — Teach your 3 non-negotiables
- **Action:** Explain your 3 non-negotiables to one person (or write them out fully).
- **Time:** 15 min
- **Do it like this:** Saying it out loud locks the habit in.

### Day 21 — FINAL Breakage Check + after photos ⭐ (Milestone 3)
- **Action:** Repeat Day 1 exactly — comb test + after photos (full length + ends). Line up
  Day 1 vs Day 21, and Day 7 / 14 / 21 wash-day piles.
- **Time:** 20 min
- **Do it like this:** Same comb, same dry/clean state, same section, one slow pass. Compare honestly.
