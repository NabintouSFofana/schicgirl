### It's not your growth. It's your retention.

Your follicles are doing their job. Type 4 hair grows roughly 1 to 1.5 cm a month from the scalp
— yours included. That part is happening whether you fuss over it or not.

The length you *see* is simply growth **minus** breakage. New hair comes in at the root. Old hair
snaps off at the ends. When breakage keeps pace with growth, your hair stalls at the same length
for months — sometimes years — and it feels like nothing is working.

This challenge attacks the side of that equation you can actually control: **the breakage.**

Less hair in the comb. Fewer snapped ends on wash day. A smaller pile at the bottom of the sink.
That's the win. That's what keeping your length actually looks like.

### How the three weeks work

**Week 1 — See it.** You'll establish your baseline: the comb test, your starting photos, and an
honest look at where breakage is happening. You can't fix what you haven't measured.

**Week 2 — Stop it.** You'll lock in the gentle-handling moves that prevent snapping — detangling
on damp, conditioner-saturated hair (never soaking wet, that's when your strands are weakest),
fingers first, then a wide-tooth comb, in four sections, working ends to roots. By your Day 14
check, the pile starts shrinking.

**Week 3 — Keep it.** You'll cement the 5-minute routine into a habit you don't have to think
about — including the small, strategic dusting that *prevents* length loss by stopping splits
before they travel up the strand and snap higher. (Trimming a little keeps you from losing a lot.
It's the opposite of losing length.)
