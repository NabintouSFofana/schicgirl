Stop the Breakage. Start keeping your length.

A 21-Day Length Retention Challenge for Type 4 hair. You're not failing at growing your hair — you're losing it at the ends. And that's fixable.

You've measured. You've waited. You've watched new growth push out every month — so why does your length look the same as last year? Here's the truth most Type 4 women are never told: your hair is growing. You're just not keeping it.
The Reframe

It's not your growth. It's your retention.

Your follicles are doing their job. Type 4 hair grows roughly 1 to 1.5 cm a month from the scalp — yours included. That part is happening whether you fuss over it or not.

Length you seewhat you keep = Growthat the root − Breakageat the ends

New hair comes in at the root. Old hair snaps off at the ends. When breakage keeps pace with growth, your hair stalls for months — sometimes years. This challenge attacks the side you can actually control: the breakage. Less hair in the comb. Fewer snapped ends on wash day. A smaller pile at the bottom of the sink. That's what keeping your length actually looks like.

How the three weeks work

See it. Stop it. Keep it.

Week 1

See it.

You'll establish your baseline: the comb test, your starting photos, and an honest look at where breakage is happening. You can't fix what you haven't measured.

Week 2

Stop it.

You'll lock in the gentle-handling moves that prevent snapping — detangling on damp, conditioner-saturated hair (never soaking wet, that's when strands are weakest), fingers first, then a wide-tooth comb, in four sections, working ends to roots. By your Day 14 check, the pile starts shrinking.

Week 3

Keep it.

You'll cement the 5-minute routine into a habit you don't have to think about — including the small, strategic dusting that prevents length loss by stopping splits before they travel up the strand and snap higher. Trimming a little keeps you from losing a lot.

The honest promise

Let's be clear, because we respect you too much not to be.

This challenge will reduce your breakage. Less hair lost on wash day. Fewer snapped ends. A 5-minute routine that finally sticks because it's built around your real wash day — weekly or every 10 to 14 days.

What this challenge will NOT do: make your hair longer in 21 days. No honest product can. Your hair grows about 1 to 1.5 cm a month from the follicle, and nothing — no oil, no challenge, no miracle — speeds that up. What you can change is how much of that growth you keep.

So we're not selling you length in three weeks. We're handing you the habit that lets you keep your length for life. That's the part nobody else will tell you straight.

Your objections, answered
"I've tried challenges before and quit."
This one is 5 minutes a day and tells you exactly what to do — no decisions, no willpower required. And if you miss a day? You don't start over from zero. You pick up where you left off.
"Will I actually see a difference in 21 days?"
Yes — in your breakage, not your length. Your Day 21 wash-day pile compared to Day 1, and your Day 21 comb test compared to Day 1, are your proof. That's a real, visible win in three weeks.
"How do I even know it's breakage and not normal shedding?"
We teach you the difference on Day 1. A broken hair is a short fragment with no white bulb. A shed hair is full-length with a white bulb at the root — and shedding 50 to 100 a day is completely normal. You'll stop panicking over the wrong thing.
"Is this just for 4C?"
It's for all of Type 4 — 4A, 4B and 4C. The principles protect every coil pattern.
